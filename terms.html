7. Cultural Sensitivity

🌱 Respecting Indigenous Knowledge

This section reflects our deep commitment to ethical engagement with indigenous cultural heritage and knowledge systems.

Nahuas.com is dedicated to presenting Nahua civilization with the respect, nuance, and dignity that all cultures deserve. We ask all users to share this commitment. When engaging with our content or sharing it with others, please observe the following principles:

  • Respect for Living Communities: The Nahua peoples are not a historical curiosity. Approximately 1.7 million Nahuatl speakers maintain vibrant cultural traditions today. Content from this site should never be used in ways that reduce living communities to museum exhibits or relics of the past.
  • Accurate Representation: When referencing or sharing our content, please maintain the accuracy and context in which it was presented. Removing context from cultural or religious information can lead to misrepresentation and harm.
  • No Commercialization of Sacred Knowledge: Certain aspects of Nahua spiritual and ceremonial life are presented here for educational understanding. This information should not be appropriated, commercialized, or used to create products, services, or performances that trivialize or exploit indigenous spiritual practices.
  • Indigenous Data Sovereignty: We recognize that indigenous communities have the right to control the collection, ownership, and application of their own cultural data. We strive to present information in ways that support, rather than undermine, this principle.
  • Avoiding Stereotypes: Content from this site should not be used to perpetuate stereotypes about indigenous peoples, whether through sensationalism, oversimplification, or decontextualization. The Nahua world was and is complex, sophisticated, and multifaceted.

We believe that learning about other cultures is one of humanity's great privileges. We ask that all users exercise this privilege with thoughtfulness and care.
